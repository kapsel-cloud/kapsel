# Choose The First Post-0.1.0 Technical Slice

Status: conditional; blocked by approved aggregate 0.1.0-use evidence.

Problem: a successful release does not authorize platform expansion. The next technical investment
must follow observed defects and use rather than repository momentum.

Contract owners: the 0.1.0 release report, [technical scope](../docs/SCOPE.md),
[architecture](../docs/ARCHITECTURE.md), and
[ADR 0008](../docs/decisions/0008-use-one-kubernetes-effect-gateway-canary.md).

Depends on: [KAP-0045](KAP-0045.md), approved aggregate technical evidence from
[KAP-0047](KAP-0047.md), and an explicit maintainer decision. Private thresholds, customer records,
and company strategy remain outside this repository.

## Inputs

- Published aggregate evaluator outcomes and integration constraints.
- Reproducible defects, security findings, support burden, and release-operability findings.
- Concrete next-capability requests tied to real workflows, when approved for technical use.
- The 0.1.0 threat model, receipt limits, and residual-risk report.

## Decision

Choose exactly one route:

1. **Stabilize** — select evidenced gaps from the future `v1.0.0` requirements in
   [technical scope](../docs/SCOPE.md) and harden the existing capability without widening scope.
2. **Specify one capability** — create one new technical owner and decision for an evidence-selected
   operation; keep it concrete before extracting any shared provider seam.
3. **Freeze** — retain security and correctness maintenance only.
4. **Retire** — archive the experiment and publish its final technical status.

## Acceptance

- The decision cites approved technical evidence and names rejected alternatives.
- No private user, buyer, market, launch, pricing, or commercial record enters the public
  repository.
- A stabilization route identifies specific `v1.0.0` assurance or compatibility gaps rather than
  pre-authorizing the tag or claiming production readiness.
- An expansion route defines exactly one operation and cannot add a generic capability interface,
  runtime plugin, hosted control plane, or second operation in the same slice.
- Shared abstractions are considered only after two concrete implementations expose a repeated seam.
- The chosen route creates a new owner and finite task sequence; all unchosen routes remain
  inactive.

## Non-goals

No standing roadmap of speculative capabilities, business continuation decision, market plan,
commercial pilot, or automatic expansion after 0.1.0.

## Next action

Remain blocked until KAP-0047 records sufficient approved aggregate technical evidence. If the
evidence is insufficient, choose freeze or retire rather than extending implementation by default.
